First, british felt that the records were to be preserved carefully.They also felt that the act of writing was important.They thought that every instruction,plan,policy,decision,agreement,investigation had to be clearly written up and preserved. Once this was done, things could be properly studied and debated.So they set up records room

attached to all administrative institution. The village tahsildar's office, the collectorate, the commissioner's office, the provincial secretary, the law court all had their record room.Specialised institutions like archives and museums were also established to preserve important records.
